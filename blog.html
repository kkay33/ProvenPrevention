Disease Prevention Basics

Preventing disease involves monitoring lifestyle choices, attending routine medical checkups, and preforming self examinations.

Preventable Disease Includes:

  • Alzheimer's Disease
  • Diabetes
  • Osteroporosis
  • Vascular Dementia
  • Pancreatitis
  • ...And Many More

How Research is Done

Scientists collect information of the lifestyles of individuals who develop certain disorders. They then look for a significant patterns in the behavior of the affected population. The results are studied in further depth to establish causation and weed out factors that are simply coorelation.

Importance

It wasn't until the 1950's when the fact that cigarettes cause cancer was broadcasted to the public. Without research on the behaviors of people developing cancer, smoking might still be accepted as healthy today. Many of our modern habits may be harmful without us knowing it. We can cut down the disease rates for the following generations by researching the behavior of our own.
